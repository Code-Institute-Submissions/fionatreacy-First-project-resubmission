Ancient Astronomy


Evidence of ancient astronomy can be seen at Newgrange during the solstices, on the shortest and longest days of the year. At sunrise for approximately 15 minutes, a beam of light illuminates the chamber of Newgrange.


This event isn't coincidental, it was built with this event in mind. The sunlight enters through a specially engineered opening in the roof, known as a roof box. This feat was achieved by groups of people in the local community with significant knowledge of astronomy, engineering and building.

Religious Significance


It's theorised that the people who built Newgrange had an astronimically-based religion. Ths would explain why they would go through the time and effort of ensuring that Newgrange was built in line with the sun during the solstices.


This theory would also assist in understanding the significance of the megalithic art found around the site. Carvings that seem to represent astral imagery such as the Belt of Orion, and spirals that could be depicting formations of stars are a common sight.


The solstice at Newgrange is still a impressive event to witness today, tickets are highly sought after. There is a lottery system in place for tickets. If you'd like to be entered please apply at the Brú Na Bóinne Visitor Centre.
