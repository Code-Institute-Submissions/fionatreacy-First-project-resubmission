Worship


Originally classified as especially grand passage tomb, Newgrange has since been recognised by archaologist to be a large temple used by Bronze age Irish people as a place of worship in the Neolithic era. Estimated to have taken 30 years and 200,000 tonnes of stone, it was central to the farming community who built it 5,200 years ago. Megalithic art can be seen all around Newgrange, large stones adorned with swirls are a common sight. The exact purpose of this art isn't known, opinions vary between it being decorative or being symbolic and religiously significant.

Burial


As well as being considered a place of worship, Newgrange was used as a tomb for the dead. Newgrange is considered to be a large passage tomb, theoretically the people of the time believed that burying their dead in this tomb would send them to the afterlife. Upon excavation, the cremated remains of several indivuduals and families were discovered. Many goods were left with the remains including marbles, pendants and beads. Originally there were probably many more people buried here, and many more gifts sent with them to the afterlife. Due to grave robbers and general degredation over time most of this has been lost to history.
